The Sol System of 2990 CE is home to approximately 5 trillion humans, distributed across numerous habitats, planetary settlements, and space stations. Several major political powers govern different regions of the system, creating a complex web of alliances, dependencies, and tensions.

After nearly a millennium of space colonization, humanity has expanded throughout the entire solar system, establishing a complex civilization with diverse cultures, technologies, and political structures.

Sol (The Sun)

Neutral Zone

The centerpiece of human civilization, Sol provides the energy that powers the vast interplanetary infrastructure. Numerous solar harvesting stations orbit at various distances, collecting energy that is beamed throughout the system via highly efficient laser networks.

Key Statistics

Solar Harvesting Stations: 1,246 major facilities
Energy Output Harvested: 0.002% of total output
Closest Permanent Facilities: Mercury L1 Research Stations

The management of solar energy resources is governed by the Sol Energy Accord, a complex agreement between all major powers that ensures equitable distribution of this vital resource. Disruption of solar collection facilities is considered an act of war against all humanity.

Mercury & Orbital Habitats

Mercurian Technological Alliance

The Mercurian Technological Alliance has established itself as a newer but rapidly advancing power, specializing in technologies adapted to extreme environments. They maintain sophisticated orbital habitats and surface operations despite Mercury's harsh conditions.

Population: 320 billion (official records)

Key Features

  • Specialized in extreme environment technologies
  • Advanced radiation shielding techniques
  • Sophisticated orbital habitats
  • Surface mining operations

Dark Secret: Unknown to the rest of the system, Mercury harbors a massive slave-based economy that would provoke system-wide intervention if discovered. This slave system provides Mercury with significant economic advantages while allowing them to bolster their official population numbers.

Earth & Luna

Terran Government

Earth gradually transitioned from being the center of human civilization to a partially rewilded world. By the 24th century, most of Earth's heavy industry had moved to space, allowing significant ecological restoration. In the current era, Earth serves primarily as a natural preserve and historical heritage site.

Earth Surface Population: 1.2 billion
Earth Orbital Habitats: 1.2 trillion

Key Features

  • Ecological restoration zones
  • Historical preservation areas
  • Limited surface settlements
  • Massive orbital habitat complexes

Earth Orbital Habitat Complexes

Terran Government

The vast majority of Earth's associated population lives in enormous orbital habitat complexes arranged in stable formations around the planet. These represent some of the oldest continuously inhabited structures in the solar system, with the oldest sections dating back to the late 21st century.

Population: 1.2 trillion

Major Habitat Clusters

  • New Terran Ring: The largest continuous habitat structure, housing over 300 billion people
  • Lagrange Complexes: Major settlements at Earth-Luna L4 and L5 points
  • Heritage Stations: Original habitats preserved as living museums

Earth orbital habitats emphasize naturalistic designs with extensive internal parklands, reflecting their cultural connection to humanity's original home.

Mars & Agricultural Zones

Martian Agricultural Federation

The Martian Agricultural Federation controls the majority of the system's food production through their terraformed agricultural zones. Mars has been partially terraformed through a patchwork of massive domes, each creating self-contained atmospheres for agricultural production.

Surface Population: 50 billion
Orbital Population: 850 billion

Key Features

  • Terraformed agricultural domes
  • Political structure based on Agricultural Zones (AZs)
  • Water rights and atmospheric processing as political leverage
  • Primary food producer for the system

Asteroid Belt

Belt Council Confederation

The Belt Council Confederation unites numerous independent habitat clusters throughout the asteroid belt. Despite significant cultural and political diversity among Belt communities, they maintain strong solidarity when dealing with other powers.

Population: 750 billion

Government Structure

Each community has representation on the council, with voting weight determined by population and resource contribution. The Belt's control over significant mineral resources and strategic positioning gives them substantial influence in system-wide politics.

Major Asteroid Clusters

  • Ceres Complex: The administrative center of the Belt
  • Vesta Mining Coalition: Resource extraction hub
  • Pallas Scientific Cooperative: Research focused settlements

Jupiter & Its Moons

Jovian Economic Alliance

The Jovian Economic Alliance has leveraged Jupiter's position and resources (particularly Helium-3 from its atmosphere) to establish itself as a financial and technological superpower. Their control over energy resources and strategic position between the inner and outer system makes them crucial players in system-wide trade and politics.

Key Features
